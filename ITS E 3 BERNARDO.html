Solo Trip to Aklan and Boracay

Last summer, I embarked on a solo trip to Aklan to visit family I hadn’t seen in years. As soon as I arrived, I was greeted with warmth and familiar smiles that made me feel instantly at home. The province had changed in little ways, but the atmosphere remained just as I remembered — calm, comforting, and filled with nature’s charm.

For the first few days, I stayed with my aunt and cousins in Kalibo. We cooked together, laughed about childhood stories, and stayed up late just talking. I also took the chance to visit the Aklan River and the Bakhawan Eco-Park. It was peaceful walking along the mangroves, listening to birds, and taking in the fresh air — a huge contrast to the city’s fast pace.

After a few days of reconnecting with my roots, we spontaneously decided to go on a quick getaway to Boracay. Even though I had been there before, returning as an adult felt different — more meaningful and exciting. The boat ride was scenic, and the moment we docked, the view of the powdery white beach took my breath away again.

We stayed in a small beachfront inn and spent our days exploring Station 1 through Station 3. Mornings were for swimming and paddleboarding. Afternoons were for lounging under umbrellas, enjoying mango shakes and grilled seafood. By night, the beach transformed with fire dancers, acoustic music, and lively laughter.

One of my favorite memories was watching the sunset from Willy’s Rock. As the sky turned orange and pink, I sat quietly and just took everything in — the waves, the breeze, and the beautiful chaos of life happening all around me. It was a moment of deep gratitude and clarity.

This trip reminded me how important it is to slow down and reconnect — both with the people you love and with yourself. Being away from the noise gave me room to breathe and reflect. I returned home feeling recharged, full of stories, and already dreaming of my next adventure.
